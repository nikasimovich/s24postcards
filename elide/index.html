Postcards of Remembrance

A Community Reading of Elie Wiesel’s Night on International Holocaust Remembrance Day

by Elide Vincenti

On Sunday January 28th, the Museum of Jewish Heritage hosted a community reading of Elie Wiesel’s seminal memoir Night. The event was orchestrated with the participation of well-known writers, authors, journalists, and notable figures like Caissie Levy, Ann Curry, John Berman, Jackie Hoffman,and others. Their reading of the entire book, accompanied by stirring music and thought-provoking speeches, infused the commemoration with added layers of significance. Furthermore, during the event, I had the distinct privilege of interviewing Mr. Joshua Mack, Vice President of Collections and Exhibitions at the Jewish Heritage Museum. Our conversation provided valuable insights into the significance of the event and shed light on the museum's dedication to preserving and sharing the rich cultural heritage.

The presence of a meditation space led by Rabbi Marc Margolius of the Institute for Jewish Spirituality provided a contemplative environment, allowing for personal introspection. The reflection wall further enhanced the experience, offering a tangible expression of collective remembrance and a poignant reminder of the importance of honoring the memories of those who suffered during the Holocaust. Overall, the event stirred a range of emotions, from somber reflection to a shared commitment to never forget the lessons of history.

Insights: A Personal Journey through Event Moments

Participating in the event was a deeply emotional experience for me. The solemnity of the occasion, combined with the weight of the words being shared, likely created a profound sense of connection and reflection. Elie Wiesel's Night is a poignant and important work that addresses the horrors of the Holocaust, and participating in a collective reading can contribute to a shared understanding and remembrance of the historical events it portrays. Reflecting on such experiences can foster a deeper appreciation for the impact of literature and the importance of remembering and learning from the past. The collective sharing of emotions and insights during the event amplified the resonance of the narrative, creating a space where personal reflections merged with the broader tapestry of historical remembrance.

"Never shall I forget that night, the first night in camp, that turned my life into one long night seven times sealed".
